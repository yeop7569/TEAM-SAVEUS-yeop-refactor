SUCCESS with gemini-2.5-flash: "OK" in Korean can be expressed in several ways, depending on the context and politeness level. Here are the most common ones:

1.  **네 (Ne)**
    *   **Meaning:** This is the most common and versatile way to say "yes" or simply acknowledge something. It often functions like "OK, I understand" or "OK, I will do it" in response to a request or question.
    *   **Usage:** Standard polite.
    *   *Example:* "Please come here." "네." (OK.)

2.  **알겠습니다 (Algesseumnida) / 알았어요 (Arasseoyo) / 알았어 (Arasseo)**
    *   **Meaning:** These phrases mean "I understand" or "I got it." They are excellent choices when you want to confirm that you've understood an instruction or information.
        *   **알겠습니다 (Algesseumnida):** Formal and very polite. Use in business or when speaking to elders/superiors.
        *   **알았어요 (Arasseoyo):** Standard polite. Use in most everyday situations.
        *   **알았어 (Arasseo):** Informal. Use with close friends, family, or people younger than you.
    *   *Example:* "The meeting is at 10 AM." "네, 알겠습니다." (OK, I understand.)

3.  **좋아요 (Joayo) / 좋아 (Joa)**
    *   **Meaning:** These mean "It's good," "Sounds good," or "I like it." Use them when you are agreeing to a suggestion, a plan, or confirming that something is acceptable.
        *   **좋아요 (Joayo):** Standard polite.
        *   **좋아 (Joa):** Informal.
    *   *Example:* "Shall we meet at 7 PM?" "좋아요!" (OK, sounds good!)

4.  **괜찮아요 (Gwaenchannayo) / 괜찮아 (Gwaenchana)**
    *   **Meaning:** These mean "It's okay," "It's fine," or "No problem." Use them when you're reassuring someone, or saying that something is acceptable or not an issue.
        *   **괜찮아요 (Gwaenchannayo):** Standard polite.
        *   **괜찮아 (Gwaenchana):** Informal.
    *   *Example:* "Is this seat OK?" "네, 괜찮아요." (Yes, it's OK.)

5.  **오케이 (Okei)**
    *   **Meaning:** This is a direct transliteration of the English word "OK." It's commonly used in informal settings, especially by younger generations or when influenced by English media.
    *   **Usage:** Informal.
    *   *Example:* "Let's go!" "오케이!" (OK!)

The best choice depends heavily on the specific context and who you are speaking to!
